 SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS SPECIFICATION Unified Social Media Agency Management Platform AI-Driven Publishing, Approval, Community Management, Asset Governance &amp; Agency Operations Document Version: 1.0 | Status: Draft for Review Prepared: July 2026 Table of Contents TOC \h \o &quot;1-3&quot; 1 Introduction 1.1 Purpose This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functional and non-functional requirements for a unified Social Media Agency Management Platform. The platform consolidates content planning, multi-platform publishing, client approval workflows, AI-driven automation, community management (Social CRM), brand/asset governance, and agency operations (profitability, capacity, white-labeling) into a single system. This document is intended for the development team, QA, project stakeholders, and prospective client-side administrators who require insight into system behavior. 1.2 Scope The system, referred to hereafter as “the Platform,” will allow marketing/social media agencies to plan, create, route for approval, publish, and monitor content across Meta (Facebook/Instagram), LinkedIn, X (Twitter), and TikTok, on behalf of multiple client brands. The Platform automates the approval and compliance chase process, centralizes inbound community engagement, enforces brand consistency via reusable Brand Kits, integrates with external creative tools (Canva, Figma, Adobe), and gives agency leadership visibility into profitability and team capacity. The Platform is delivered as a multi-tenant SaaS web application with role-based, white-labeled client portals. 1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, and Abbreviations Term Definition Agency The tenant organization using the Platform to manage one or more Clients. Client A brand/business whose social accounts are managed by the Agency. Workspace An isolated container of campaigns, assets, and users scoped to a single Client. Asset Any raw or finished media file (video, image, audio, copy) stored in the Media Library. Post A scheduled or published piece of content targeted at one or more social platforms. Approval Workflow A configurable, ordered sequence of stakeholders who must review/approve a Post before it publishes. SRS Software Requirements Specification. RBAC Role-Based Access Control. SLA Service Level Agreement (e.g., a 24-hour approval window). Global Inbox The unified feed of DMs, comments, mentions, and tags across all connected platforms. Brand Kit The stored set of a Client&apos;s colors, typography, tone-of-voice, and compliance rules. 1.4 References IEEE Std 830-1998 – Recommended Practice for Software Requirements Specifications Meta Graph API, LinkedIn Marketing API, X API v2, TikTok for Business API documentation GDPR (EU 2016/679) and applicable regional data-protection regulations WCAG 2.1 AA Accessibility Guidelines 1.5 Document Overview Section 2 describes the product at a high level, including sections A–C which establish the platform foundation. Section 8 provides a requirements traceability matrix, and Section 9 lists assumptions, dependencies, and open issues. 2 Overall Description 2.1 Product Perspective The Platform is a new, standalone, multi-tenant SaaS product. It integrates with external social platform APIs (Meta, LinkedIn, X, TikTok), external creative tools (Canva, Figma, Adobe), and communication channels (email, Slack) for automated notifications. It does not replace those platforms&apos; native analytics but aggregates and orchestrates activity across them from a single agency-facing and client-facing interface. 2.2 Product Functions (Summary) A – Platform Overview &amp; Core Architecture: multi-tenant workspace foundation, authentication, and content calendar shell. B – Multi-Platform Content Calendar &amp; Publishing Engine: drafting, scheduling, and cross-platform publishing of Posts. C – Client Collaboration &amp; Baseline Approval Workflow: manual review, commenting, and sign-off between Agency and Client. D – AI-Driven Workflow &amp; Approval Automation: intelligent routing, automated chasing, and brand-safety/duplicate checks layered on top of B and C. E – Unified Social CRM: global inbox, AI sentiment triage, and reply-collision prevention. F – Centralized Asset &amp; Brand Management: tagged media library, Brand Kits, and creative-tool integrations. G – Agency Operations &amp; Capacity Planning: profitability tracking, white-labeled portals, and RBAC. 2.3 User Classes and Characteristics User Class Description Agency Owner / Admin Full access across all Client workspaces; sees global revenue, capacity, and pipeline metrics; configures RBAC and white-label branding. Account Manager Manages one or more Client relationships; configures approval workflows; monitors chasing/escalations. Creator / Designer Uploads and tags assets, drafts Posts, pushes files from Canva/Figma/Adobe into the queue. Copywriter Drafts post copy assisted by the Brand Kit AI voice assistant. Legal / Compliance Reviewer Mandatory reviewer for regulated content (e.g., healthcare, finance clients) prior to Client visibility. Client Stakeholder External brand representative who approves/rejects Posts via a white-labeled portal. Community Manager Monitors and responds to the Global Inbox; triages sentiment-flagged items. Freelancer Restricted user who sees only explicitly assigned campaigns (RBAC-scoped). 2.4 Operating Environment Web application accessible via modern evergreen browsers (Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox); responsive layout supporting tablet use for on-the-go approvals; backend deployed on cloud infrastructure with horizontal scalability; mobile-responsive client approval portal (native app out of scope for v1). 2.5 Design and Implementation Constraints Must operate within third-party API rate limits imposed by Meta, LinkedIn, X, and TikTok. Regulated-industry Clients (e.g., healthcare, finance, pharma) require an immutable, auditable compliance trail. White-labeling requires custom-domain SSL provisioning per Agency. Must comply with GDPR and applicable regional data-protection law for stored personal data (DMs, comments). 2.6 Assumptions and Dependencies Clients and Agencies will grant the Platform the necessary OAuth permissions/API access to their social accounts. Availability and rate limits of third-party social APIs are outside the Platform&apos;s control and may constrain real-time behavior (e.g., Global Inbox latency). Canva, Figma, and Adobe expose stable public APIs/plugins suitable for direct asset hand-off. 3 Specific Requirements Each requirement is uniquely identified (module prefix + number) and assigned a priority: High (must-have for v1), Medium (should-have), Low (nice-to-have / phase 2). “Shall” denotes a mandatory, testable requirement. 3.1 Section A — Platform Overview &amp; Core Architecture Establishes the multi-tenant foundation that all other modules build upon. ID Requirement Priority FR-A-1 The system shall support multi-tenancy such that each Agency&apos;s data, users, and Clients are logically isolated from other Agencies. High FR-A-2 The system shall allow an Agency Admin to create a Workspace per Client, scoping campaigns, assets, and users to that Client. High FR-A-3 The system shall provide a unified content calendar (day/week/month/list views) showing all Posts across a Client&apos;s connected platforms. High FR-A-4 The system shall support authentication via email/password and single sign-on (SSO/SAML) for enterprise Agencies. Medium FR-A-5 The system shall maintain an immutable audit log of all create, edit, approve, reject, and publish actions. High 3.2 Section B — Multi-Platform Content Calendar &amp; Publishing Engine Covers drafting, scheduling, and native publishing of content to connected social accounts. ID Requirement Priority FR-B-1 The system shall allow a Creator to draft a Post with platform-specific variants (copy length, aspect ratio, hashtags) for Meta, LinkedIn, X, and TikTok from a single draft. High FR-B-2 The system shall allow a Post to be scheduled for a specific date/time per target platform, respecting each platform&apos;s optimal-posting-time recommendations where available. High FR-B-3 The system shall publish approved Posts automatically at their scheduled time via each platform&apos;s native API. High FR-B-4 The system shall retry a failed publish attempt at least twice and alert the responsible Account Manager on final failure. High FR-B-5 The system shall tag every Post with metadata (Client, campaign, content type, industry/vertical) at creation time. High 3.3 Section C — Client Collaboration &amp; Baseline Approval Workflow Defines the manual review/approve/reject mechanics that Section D automates and augments. ID Requirement Priority FR-C-1 The system shall allow a Client Stakeholder to view Posts pending their approval in a dedicated portal. High FR-C-2 The system shall allow reviewers to approve, reject, or comment inline on a specific Post (with optional annotation on the asset itself). High FR-C-3 The system shall notify the Creator/Account Manager immediately upon rejection, including the reviewer&apos;s comment. High FR-C-4 The system shall support a configurable multi-step approval chain (e.g., Account Manager → Legal → Client) per Client or per content tag. High FR-C-5 The system shall prevent a Post from publishing until all required steps in its approval chain are complete. High 3.4 Section D — AI-Driven Workflow &amp; Approval Automation Removes manual chasing by having the platform intelligently route content and self-manage the approval SLA. ID Requirement Priority FR-D-1 The system shall automatically determine the approval route for a new Asset/Post based on its tags (e.g., Client industry, content type) at upload time. High FR-D-2 The system shall route Posts tagged with a regulated industry (e.g., “Healthcare Client”, “Financial Client”) through a mandatory Legal/Compliance review step before the Client sees the draft. High FR-D-3 The system shall allow routine, untagged, or pre-approved-category Posts to bypass intermediate review and route directly to the Client. Medium FR-D-4 The system shall monitor the time elapsed since a Post entered a pending-approval state relative to its scheduled publish time. High FR-D-5 The system shall automatically send a reminder (email and/or Slack) to the outstanding approver if a Post remains unapproved 24 hours before its scheduled launch, without requiring human initiation. High FR-D-6 The system shall escalate to the Account Manager (and optionally the Agency Admin) if a Post remains unapproved within a configurable threshold (e.g., 4 hours) of scheduled launch. Medium FR-D-7 The system shall scan draft copy against a Client-specific banned/off-brand terminology list and flag violations before Client visibility. High FR-D-8 The system shall detect Posts missing a required legal disclaimer (as configured per Client/industry) and block submission until resolved. High FR-D-9 The system shall detect and flag duplicate or near-duplicate scheduled Posts (same asset/copy, same platform, overlapping time window) before they reach the Client. Medium 3.5 Section E — Unified Social CRM (Community Management) ID Requirement Priority FR-E-1 The system shall aggregate DMs, comments, tags, and mentions from Meta, LinkedIn, X, and TikTok into a single Global Inbox per Client. High FR-E-2 The system shall allow filtering/sorting the Global Inbox by platform, Client, sentiment, and status (open/resolved). High FR-E-3 The system shall run sentiment analysis on inbound messages/comments and classify them (e.g., Positive, Neutral, Negative/High Priority). High FR-E-4 The system shall surface messages classified as High Priority (angry sentiment, PR risk keywords) at the top of the queue and notify assigned Community Managers. High FR-E-5 The system shall draft an auto-reply suggestion for messages matching known routine FAQ patterns (e.g., business hours, location), pending human send-approval by default. Medium FR-E-6 The system shall prevent two Agency users from replying to the same comment/DM simultaneously by locking the item to the first user who opens it, with visible presence indicators. High FR-E-7 The system shall release a lock automatically if the item is left inactive beyond a configurable timeout (e.g., 5 minutes). Medium 3.6 Section F — Centralized Asset &amp; Brand Management ID Requirement Priority FR-F-1 The system shall provide a cloud Media Library where raw and finished video, image, and audio files are stored and tagged by Client and campaign. High FR-F-2 The system shall support full-text and tag-based search/filtering across the Media Library. High FR-F-3 The system shall store a Brand Kit per Client containing hex color palette, typography, logo files, and tone-of-voice guidelines. High FR-F-4 The system shall expose the active Client&apos;s Brand Kit to the copywriting AI assistant so drafted copy matches the Client&apos;s tone of voice. High FR-F-5 The system shall integrate with Canva, Figma, and Adobe (via API/plugin) allowing a Creator to push a finished design directly into the Platform&apos;s scheduling queue without manual download/re-upload. Medium FR-F-6 The system shall version finished Assets pushed from an external design tool, retaining prior versions for audit/history. Low 3.7 Section G — Agency Operations &amp; Capacity Planning ID Requirement Priority FR-G-1 The system shall allow Agency staff to log time spent per Client workspace (manually or via integrated time-tracking). High FR-G-2 The system shall compare logged time cost against a Client&apos;s monthly retainer value to compute a profitability/margin indicator per Client. High FR-G-3 The system shall flag Clients experiencing “scope creep” when logged effort exceeds retainer-covered hours by a configurable threshold. Medium FR-G-4 The system shall allow an Agency Admin to configure white-label branding (logo, color scheme, custom domain) applied to the Client-facing portal. High FR-G-5 The system shall enforce Role-Based Access Control so Freelancers see only explicitly assigned campaigns/Clients, while Agency Owners see global revenue, capacity, and pipeline metrics. High FR-G-6 The system shall provide an Agency-wide capacity dashboard showing team workload (hours logged/allocated) across all active Clients. Medium 4 Use Cases UC-1 — Intelligent Routing of a Regulated Asset Field Description Actor(s) Creator, Legal Reviewer, System Preconditions Creator has upload permission; Client tagging taxonomy is configured (e.g., “Healthcare Client”). Trigger Creator uploads a new video Asset and tags it “Healthcare Client, Reel.” Main Flow 1) System reads tags. 2) System detects a regulated-industry tag. 3) System routes the draft Post to the Legal/Compliance queue instead of directly to the Client. 4) Legal reviewer approves or rejects with comments. 5) On approval, system forwards the Post to the Client for final sign-off. Alternate / Exception Flow If Legal rejects, the Post returns to the Creator with comments and does not advance to the Client. Postcondition Post is either published-ready (fully approved) or returned for revision, with full routing history logged. UC-2 — Automated Chasing of an Unapproved Post Field Description Actor(s) System, Client Stakeholder, Account Manager Preconditions Post is scheduled with a defined launch time and is awaiting Client approval. Trigger Scheduled launch time is within 24 hours and the Post is still pending. Main Flow 1) System detects the 24-hour threshold. 2) System sends an automated reminder via email and/or Slack to the outstanding approver. 3) If still unapproved at the configured escalation threshold, system escalates to the Account Manager. Alternate / Exception Flow If the Post is approved before the threshold, no reminder is sent. Postcondition Approver is reminded without human intervention; escalation is logged if the SLA continues to be missed. UC-3 — Sentiment Triage in the Global Inbox Field Description Actor(s) System, Community Manager Preconditions Client&apos;s social accounts are connected; sentiment model is active. Trigger A new comment or DM arrives on any connected platform. Main Flow 1) System ingests the message into the Global Inbox. 2) System runs sentiment classification. 3) If classified Negative/High Priority, item is surfaced at top of queue and Community Manager is notified. 4) If it matches a routine FAQ pattern, system drafts a suggested reply for human approval. Alternate / Exception Flow If sentiment is ambiguous, item is flagged Neutral and queued for standard review. Postcondition High-risk messages receive prompt human attention; routine messages are answered faster via AI-drafted replies. UC-4 — Collision Prevention on a Shared Comment Field Description Actor(s) Two Community Managers, System Preconditions Two Agency users have access to the same Client&apos;s inbox. Trigger User A opens a comment thread to reply. Main Flow 1) System locks the item to User A and shows a presence indicator to other users. 2) User B attempts to open the same item and sees it is locked, with User A&apos;s name. 3) User A replies and closes the item, releasing the lock. Alternate / Exception Flow If User A is inactive beyond the timeout, the lock auto-releases and User B may claim it. Postcondition Only one reply is sent per comment; duplicate/conflicting replies are prevented. UC-5 — Brand-Voice-Assisted Copywriting Field Description Actor(s) Copywriter, System Preconditions Client&apos;s Brand Kit (tone-of-voice, terminology) is configured. Trigger Copywriter opens a new Post draft for a specific Client. Main Flow 1) System loads the Client&apos;s Brand Kit. 2) Copywriter requests AI-suggested copy. 3) System generates copy aligned to the stored tone-of-voice and flags any off-brand terminology in the draft. Alternate / Exception Flow If no Brand Kit exists for the Client, system prompts the Copywriter to proceed without AI suggestions or create one first. Postcondition Draft copy is on-brand and ready for the approval workflow. UC-6 — Profitability &amp; Scope-Creep Review Field Description Actor(s) Agency Owner, System Preconditions Time entries have been logged against the Client workspace for the billing period. Trigger Agency Owner opens the Client Profitability dashboard. Main Flow 1) System aggregates logged hours and converts to cost. 2) System compares cost to the Client&apos;s monthly retainer value. 3) System displays margin and flags the Client if logged effort exceeds the retainer-covered threshold. Alternate / Exception Flow If time-tracking integration is unavailable, system falls back to manually logged entries only and indicates data may be incomplete. Postcondition Agency Owner can identify under- or over-serviced Clients for renegotiation or resourcing decisions. 5 Logical Data Model The core entities below capture the primary objects the Platform must persist. Webhooks from social platforms shall be verified via signature validation before processing. 7 Non-Functional Requirements 7.1 Performance NFR-1: The content calendar shall load a Client&apos;s month view of Posts within 2 seconds under normal load. NFR-2: The Global Inbox shall reflect a new inbound platform message within 60 seconds of receipt via API/webhook, subject to third-party API rate limits. NFR-3: The system shall support at least 50 concurrent Agency users and 500 scheduled Posts per Client workspace without degraded performance. 7.2 Security &amp; Compliance NFR-4: All personal data (DMs, comments, contact details) shall be encrypted at rest and in transit. NFR-5: The system shall comply with GDPR data-subject access and deletion requirements. NFR-6: The audit log (FR-A-5) shall be tamper-evident and retained for a minimum of 24 months for regulated Clients. NFR-7: OAuth tokens for connected social accounts shall be stored encrypted and scoped to least-privilege permissions. 7.3 Availability &amp; Reliability NFR-8: The Platform shall target 99.5% uptime for publishing and approval-critical services. NFR-9: Scheduled Posts shall not be lost on transient failure; failed publishes shall be retried per FR-B-4 and never silently dropped. 7.4 Usability &amp; Accessibility NFR-10: The Client Portal shall conform to WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility guidelines. NFR-11: White-label theming shall not compromise minimum color-contrast accessibility thresholds. 7.5 Scalability &amp; Maintainability NFR-12: The system architecture shall allow new social platforms to be added as publishing/inbox connectors without core data-model changes. NFR-13: AI components (sentiment analysis, routing, copy assistant, duplicate detection) shall be modular services that can be independently retrained/updated. 8 Requirements Traceability Matrix Maps each module&apos;s requirements to the primary stakeholder(s) who requested/benefit from it and the related use case(s), for QA and stakeholder sign-off tracing.
